Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, no moons, and is covered in craters.

Venus

Venus has a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide, making it the hottest planet. It rotates in the opposite direction to most planets.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with vast oceans, an oxygen-rich atmosphere, and a diverse ecosystem.

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, has the largest volcano in the solar system and signs of ancient water sources.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet, with a massive storm called the Great Red Spot and at least 79 known moons.

Saturn

Saturn is known for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock. It has at least 83 moons.

Uranus

Uranus rotates on its side and has a pale blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun, with supersonic winds and a deep blue appearance.
